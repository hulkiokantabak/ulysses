Ulysses Project

One day — Thursday, 16 June 1904, Dublin — and one first cover-to-cover reading of the book that holds it: James Joyce's Ulysses, read 7–17 July 2026, Penguin Modern Classics, introduction and notes by Declan Kiberd (1922 text). This site is built out of that reading. It is not a blog and not a single essay; it grows, most of it is not built yet, and it says so honestly.

What is here now

  • Everyone in Ulysses, and the day they cross — the interactive ring: 51 characters, four circles, the book's eighteen hours.
  • The map and the day — Dublin, both routes, and the time rail.
  • The reading itself — the primary record: the full 117-page Q&A of the read, the questions-only set, and the Four Odysseys comparative.
  • The reference documents — the characters, the world and city of 1904, and the itinerary of place and hour.

The two entrances

The rest of the project is analytic: up to sixteen planned studies of the book, each of which may end up as a web page, a PDF, a slide deck, or a diagram. In the project's own vocabulary: sixteen dimensions, organised by seven types of operation, each earning output in up to four registers. That is a grid, and it can be entered from either edge.

  • By dimension — one thing, in whatever forms it exists.
  • By register — every document, every deck, every diagram, across the whole project.

The method records how all of this is being made — the four registers, the seven types, the advisors, and the project's own log of errors.
