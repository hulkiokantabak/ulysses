Ulysses Project

A website and a presentation built out of a first cover-to-cover reading of James Joyce's Ulysses — 8–17 July 2026, Penguin Modern Classics, introduction and notes by Declan Kiberd (1922 text). It is not a blog and not a single essay: it holds a primary reading record, three base reference documents and two interactive artifacts, up to sixteen analytic dimensions organised by seven types of operation, a two-deck presentation, and a published method section. It grows; most of it is not built yet, and the site says so honestly.

The two entrances

Every dimension has up to four registers — HTML, PDF, presentation, diagrams. That is a grid, and it can be entered from either edge.

  • By dimension — one thing, in whatever forms it exists.
  • By register — every document, every deck, every diagram, across the whole project.

What is here now

  • The reading — the primary record: the full 117-page Q&A of the read, the questions-only set, and the Four Odysseys comparative.
  • The reference documents — the characters, the world and city of 1904, and the itinerary of place and hour — with the interactive ring, and the map and time rail.
  • The method — how this is being made: the four registers, the seven types, the advisors, and the project's own log of errors.
