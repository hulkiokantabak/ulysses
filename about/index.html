About

The reading

One first cover-to-cover reading of James Joyce's Ulysses from 7th to 17th of July 2026, in eleven days and sixteen logged sessions on Claude (followed by parallel session in ChatGPT). Everything on this site descends from that reading and from the documents built while the book was fresh.

The reader is Hulki Okan Tabak.

The edition

Penguin Modern Classics, introduction and notes by Declan Kiberd (1922 text). The reference documents cite by episode rather than page, so they survive an edition change. Joyce printed no episode titles — Telemachus, Calypso and the rest come from schemas he circulated privately and then contradicted between versions; where this project uses them, it uses them as scaffolding and says so (anchor A2).

Rights note — read before publishing anything

The site is public. The repository is private, but everything that reaches the site is published. Three lines govern what may go up.

1. Ulysses (1922) is in the public domain. Quotation from the novel is unrestricted. Quote it freely and at length.

2. The apparatus around it is not. Declan Kiberd's introduction and notes in the Penguin Modern Classics edition are in copyright, as are Gifford's Ulysses Annotated, the Slote/Mamigonian/Turner Annotations, and every other secondary work this project cites. Paraphrase them in our own words and cite the source. Do not reproduce their text, and do not reconstruct a source's argument point by point in a way that substitutes for reading it.

3. Dimension 16's sources are reviews of a 2026 film. The film, its stills and its script are in copyright, and so is every review the comparison rests on. Paraphrase and cite; publish no stills without a licence; quote no review at length.

Practical rule for the build: anything on the site that is not Joyce's 1922 text or our own writing carries an attribution line. If it can't be attributed, it doesn't go up.
