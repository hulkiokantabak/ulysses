The Object Museum

This dimension is being built now.

Chair's pick of the Type-I three (anchor A11), third in the build order. The presentation and diagram registers remain; the PDF register is skipped by budget.

The claim
Thirty-two objects, followed materially through the day, carry its structures — post, coin, memory, empire, desire, the body. Followed hand to hand, the book's plot is also a logistics.
The unit
the object (~30) — position hour by hour, hand to hand
What it shows
The day's paper economy outweighs its coin: post, print and pasteboard fill more than a third of the museum. Its most governing object is an absence — the latchkey left in the other trousers, ruling the day from eight in the morning to two the next. And its longest itinerary is fourpence of unpaid credit: the lemon soap, pocket to pocket from morning to the small hours, used at the sink still unpaid for.
