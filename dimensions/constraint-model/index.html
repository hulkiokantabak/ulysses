The Constraint Model

Dimension 13 · Type V, Model (a constructed system you operate) · Build status: building · Panel score: not yet scored

This dimension is being built now.

Routed through the game-design lenses per the working document; adversarially verified twice before publication. Built to its HTML and — 19 July 2026 — its document PDF; the constraint-graph diagram is the one register that remains. The PDF states in its own voice that a printed document cannot be operated: it is the model's record, not its substitute.

The claim
Operated as a routing-and-avoidance problem, the day's shape is structural rather than arranged. Bloom's constraints are mostly about where he must not be; Stephen's single missing key compounds hour by hour; and the near-misses need no novelist to arrange them.
The unit
the constraint — a graph of eighteen places from the itinerary, a clock running from eight, two purses in pre-decimal coin, and the obligations of two men
What it shows
Run the day yourself and nearly every Bloom decision is a decision not to be somewhere; run a thousand random days through the same graph and they cross the documented track again and again — the book's most-praised coincidences sit inside the expected range of two men with overlapping errands in a small city. A toy model in the economist's sense: it claims nothing about what anyone felt or intended, and says so in its own voice.
