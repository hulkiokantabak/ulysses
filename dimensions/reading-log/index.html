The Reading Log

Dimension 15 · Type VII, Instrument (a tool a reader uses on their own read) · Build status: built · Panel score: not yet scored

Built 19 July 2026, the same day anchor A15 was discharged (catch C-27). Type VII: the instrument IS the register, and the whole of it. Self-contained per the author's ruling C-22 — no server, no account, no analytics, no network code of any kind; entries live in the reader's own browser on the reader's own device, and can be printed or saved by the reader as a file on their own disk. The project receives nothing, because there is nowhere for anything to go. Verified in a browser before publication: entries persist across reload, free text is escaped so a pasted script cannot run, and the file contains no network call.

The claim
The instrument that measured one reading can be published rather than kept, so that any first reader can keep the same kind of record of their own. Dimension 11 turned inside out: not the result, the apparatus.
The unit
the reading session — date, pages, where the reader stalled, what they wanted to ask, what unblocked them, and whether they stopped
What it shows
That the prompts of a difficulty log are an empirical question, not a theoretical one. These eight come from what one real accompanied first reading actually produced, not from a model of where a reader ought to struggle — and the ninth kind that reading produced is deliberately absent, because it was an artefact of dictation and does not generalise. The instrument states that exclusion in its own voice rather than quietly dropping it.

The artifact

  • The Reading Log — the instrument, for your own first reading (HTML) — Built 19 July 2026, once anchor A15 was discharged (catch C-27). Its prompts are dimension 11's kinds of difficulty — eight of the nine, the ninth left out because it was an artefact of that reader's dictation and does not generalise, which the instrument says in its own voice. Entirely self-contained per C-22: it has no server, no account, no analytics and no network code of any kind. Entries are held by the reader's own browser on the reader's own device, and the project receives nothing — not the entries, not a count of them, not the fact that the page was opened.

In the reading

  • Its prompts descend from dimension 11's taxonomy of difficulty, which was collected from the 117-page reading record — this dimension has no reading anchors of its own, because it collects nothing. — /dimensions/difficulty-curve/

Sources

  • Dimension 11, the Difficulty Curve, is the sole source: its nine kinds of difficulty, of which eight are offered here as prompts.

Confidence

The instrument makes no claim at all — it is a tool, not a finding, and it produces no data this project will ever see. The one judgement inside it is the exclusion of the ninth kind (dictation and transcription noise) as specific to how the source record was made rather than to reading Ulysses; that judgement is stated in the instrument itself, where a reader can disagree with it.
