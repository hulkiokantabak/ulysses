The Atlas of One Day

Dimension 14 · Type VI, Made object (a work, not a document about a work) · Build status: planned · Panel score: not yet scored

This dimension is planned and has not been built. The empty sections below are empty because nothing has been made yet, not because something is hidden.

Rescoped by the author, 19 July 2026: built from publicly available material only, with a credits page carrying every source, licence and URL — and if a rights problem could not be avoided, the dimension was to be struck. It can be avoided, so it stands (catch C-32). **The caption-free form turns out to be what makes the licensing lawful, not what threatens it**: CC BY 4.0 permits attribution to live in a colophon rather than beside the image, and CC BY-SA 2.0's clause requiring credit 'at least as prominent as' any other authorship credit is satisfied vacuously when no panel carries any credit at all. Warburg's refusal of the caption and the licence compliance are the same decision — which also means that adding one tasteful signature to a panel would break every share-alike licence on it at once. Dropped as uncleanable: the newspaper page (no free source exists for a 16 June 1904 Dublin page image; the Evening Telegraph masthead on Commons substitutes), Joyce's handwriting and any manuscript image (the Rosenbach grants no permission, and unpublished Joyce stays in copyright in the UK until 2039), photographic portraits of Joyce, and the separate tram map (Thom's 1904 carries the tram information already). Still the strongest candidate for the project's eventual physical form.

Sources

  • Wikimedia Commons, per file and per licence — the place layer. Period photographs come from the Commons re-hosts of the Lawrence Collection, restricted to files attributed to Robert French (d. 24 June 1917, so public domain in Ireland and the EU since 1 January 1988); contemporary photographs from files under CC0, CC BY or CC BY-SA, with Irish freedom of panorama (CRRA 2000 s.93) clearing the architecture underneath.
  • Thom's Official Directory of Ireland, 1904 — the 61st annual publication, the volume Joyce himself worked from — digitised at 600dpi by the University of Victoria. Public domain by age; the holding institution asserts no licence, only a 'No Known Copyright' statement, which is a statement of its knowledge and not a warranty. The clearance rests on the age of a 1904 commercial compilation, and the credits page says so as reasoning rather than citing it as a licence.
  • Ordnance Survey six-inch sheets of Dublin, c.1900 — Crown copyright expired. To be sourced from the National Library of Scotland's series via the Digital Repository of Ireland, NOT from Tailte Éireann, whose terms require a copyright permit for its own scans. The governing licence line is UNREAD and is the dimension's first gate.
  • The 1922 text of Ulysses — public domain in Ireland and the EU since 1 January 2012, and in the United States.

Confidence

A rights survey of four material classes, each conclusion adversarially challenged, was run on 19 July 2026 (catch C-32) after the author rescoped the dimension to publicly available material. Its load-bearing finding: **an institution's terms are not the copyright.** The National Library of Ireland and Tailte Éireann both assert re-use conditions over their own scans, but neither holds copyright in a French photograph or a Victorian Ordnance Survey sheet. The remedy is therefore sourcing, not permission — the same image is taken from a repository that imposes no such terms. Every image will carry a named licence at a named URL on the credits page, and nothing enters the dimension without one. **Two gates remain and the dimension is not buildable until both are cleared.** (1) The object layer — the third of the three registers — has not been surveyed at all; no item has been cleared, and if eighteen objects cannot each be given a licence, the panel falls from three registers to two. (2) The Ordnance Survey licence line has not been read by anyone: a peer-reviewed paper records the sheets being supplied to the Digital Repository of Ireland under CC-BY, but the repository returned 403 and the National Library of Scotland's terms page returned 405, so this rests on a description of the arrangement rather than on the arrangement itself. Neither gate is a rights problem; both are unfinished work, and they are named here so the dimension is not begun on the assumption that they are closed.
