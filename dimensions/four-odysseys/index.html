Four Odysseys

Dimension 16 · Type IV, Comparative (the book against other versions of itself) · Build status: built · Panel score: not yet scored

The project's first fully built dimension: all three budgeted registers exist — the PDF handoff, the two-axis chart (diagram), and the audience deck (presentation). Supplies dimension 9's analytic frame — refusing plot and refusing register are different acts.

The claim
Fidelity to Homer's plot and interior orientation are two separable axes, usually collapsed into one. Collapsed, the four Odysseys line up on a single scale of faithfulness; separated, they stop lining up at all.
The unit
the work, measured on two axes — fidelity to Homer's plot and genre, and interior orientation (Homer · Wilson 2017 · Nolan 2026 · Joyce 1922)
What it shows
Wilson and Joyce share an anti-mystification impulse — both remove something (archaism; heroic distance) rather than add, while Nolan adds. Wilson de-mystifies the sentence; Nolan de-mystifies the hero but keeps the myth; Joyce discards the myth's events and re-mystifies the ordinary. Three different kinds of empathy — not one scale of it. A sharper test of the two axes arrived with the July 2026 reporting: at the hanging of the slave girls, Wilson refuses to look away and renders it flatly, while Nolan is reported to remove the scene altogether, displacing it onto a single betrayer. The same event, and the translator is the one who will not soften it.

Confidence

Every claim about Nolan's film is second-hand: the original handoff rested on day-one reviews, and the July 2026 reporting added since (relayed through admitted commentary held in the library, citing Vanity Fair, GQ, TODAY.com) is fuller but still not the film. Confidence markings — High / Medium / Partial / Unconfirmed — survive into anything published (A5), and the chart still marks Nolan's placement as resting on reports. Of the four open questions, one has narrowed and three stand: Calypso's arc is now reported as erasure then release — she gives the lotus so he forgets, and lets him go once he remembers what he is returning to — which narrows but does not close it; the Cattle of the Sun resolution and the Laestrygonian child's gender remain thin; and whether Nolan has ever mentioned Joyce is still unevidenced — the admitted commentary asserts that Nolan reads Homer through Joyce, but that is the commentator's interpretation, not a source, and it closes nothing. Three newly reported departures — Sinon imported from Virgil's Aeneid in place of Achilles, the elided hanging of the slave girls, and an ending in which Odysseus is exiled rather than celebrated — all push Nolan's fidelity toward the low edge of the stated ~70%; the marker has not been moved, because second-hand evidence does not earn that precision.
