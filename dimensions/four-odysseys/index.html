Four Odysseys

The claim
Fidelity to Homer's plot and interior orientation are two separable axes, usually collapsed into one. Collapsed, the four Odysseys line up on a single scale of faithfulness; separated, they stop lining up at all.
The unit
the work, measured on two axes — fidelity to Homer's plot and genre, and interior orientation (Homer · Wilson 2017 · Nolan 2026 · Joyce 1922)
What it shows
Wilson and Joyce share an anti-mystification impulse — both remove something (archaism; heroic distance) rather than add, while Nolan adds. Wilson de-mystifies the sentence; Nolan de-mystifies the hero but keeps the myth; Joyce discards the myth's events and re-mystifies the ordinary. Three different kinds of empathy — not one scale of it. A sharper test of the two axes: at the hanging of the slave girls, Wilson refuses to look away and renders it flatly — checkable in her own book — while Nolan is reported to remove the scene altogether, displacing it onto a single betrayer. The same event, and the translator is the one who will not soften it. Note the asymmetry of the evidence: Wilson's half is verifiable, Nolan's half is second-hand.

Confidence

Every claim about Nolan's film is second-hand, and the later commentary does NOT corroborate the earlier handoff: both rest on the same July 2026 journalism (Vanity Fair, GQ, TODAY.com), so agreement between them is one ancestor counted twice, not two witnesses. Nothing has been promoted on the grounds that two documents agree. Two further limits on the admitted commentary: its bibliography is a block list, so no individual claim in it is traceable to a particular source; and it presents nine departures where its own cited Vanity Fair piece names ten, so its selection is editorial. The one claim in it that would matter most — that Wilson has confirmed she was not consulted by Nolan — cannot be supported from its own citations, whose only Wilson interview predates the film by nine years; it is held at Medium until the interview is found. A correction, 19 July 2026 (catch C-28): this field previously called two facts about Wilson's translation — the strict iambic pentameter line-for-line across 12,110 lines, and the use of 'slave' where older translations say 'maid' — High and 'independent of any journalism', on the grounds that they are checkable against Wilson's own book. They are checkable. They were not checked. Both trace, in this repository, only to the admitted third-model commentary in the library — the same document whose bibliography this field elsewhere describes as a block list in which no individual claim is traceable. Asserting a verification that was never performed is the exact failure L-13 was written about, committed inside the paragraph warning against it. Both facts are therefore held at Medium, marked as relayed, until someone opens the translation and confirms them. The dimension's central finding rests on them, so this is not a small marking. Confidence markings — High / Medium / Partial / Unconfirmed — survive into anything published (A5), and the chart still marks Nolan's placement as resting on reports. Of the four open questions, one has narrowed and three stand: Calypso's arc is now reported as erasure then release — she gives the lotus so he forgets, and lets him go once he remembers what he is returning to — which narrows but does not close it; the Cattle of the Sun resolution and the Laestrygonian child's gender remain thin; and whether Nolan has ever mentioned Joyce is still unevidenced — the admitted commentary asserts that Nolan reads Homer through Joyce, but that is the commentator's interpretation, not a source, and it closes nothing. Three newly reported departures — Sinon imported from Virgil's Aeneid in place of Achilles, the elided hanging of the slave girls, and an ending in which Odysseus is exiled rather than celebrated — all push Nolan's fidelity toward the low edge of the stated ~70%; the marker has not been moved, because second-hand evidence does not earn that precision.
