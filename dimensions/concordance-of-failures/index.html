The Concordance of Failures

Dimension 9 · Type II, Inversion (map the absence rather than the presence) · Build status: building · Panel score: -2

This dimension is being built now.

Built to its PDF register 19 July 2026; the audience deck is the register that remains. The project's only Type II inversion, and it survived on Cortazar's objection that an inventory is not a dimension. Its six candidate rows arrived from admitted commentary and NONE of them was used as evidence — every row was rebuilt from the 1922 text under learning L-13, because a refusal claimed from commentary is not a refusal shown.

The claim
Ulysses does not secularise the Odyssey by deletion. Across eighteen refusals verified against the 1922 text, not one Homeric element is simply removed: every refusal keeps the Homeric occasion on the page and reassigns the power that made it work — downward into a person who has none, or outward into something that is not a person at all.
The unit
the place where Joyce refuses Homer — one column, eighteen rows
What it shows
The divine is never subtracted from Ulysses; it is relocated, and it lands either below the human or beyond it, never at the human scale where Homer keeps it — Athena demoted to a crone taking a coin, the shades' speech handed to a gramophone, Zeus's bolt handed to the weather and glossed by an ad-canvasser. And in nine of the eighteen the refusal is transacted: the Homeric obligation is not performed but discharged — settled, priced, itemised, paid off — through both terminal positions of the book and through the position of the slaughter of the suitors, which the book grades as a schedule of remedies and ranks hushmoney above assassination. Where Homer settles with an act, Ulysses settles with an account. Its formula for refusing Homer is 'We're square.'

The artifact

  • The Concordance of Failures — eighteen refusals (hyperlinked PDF) (PDF) — Built 19 July 2026. Six candidate refusals reached this project from commentary generated by another model; under its own discipline none of them counted, because a refusal claimed from commentary is not a refusal shown. Every one of the eighteen rows is anchored in a quotation grepped out of the 1922 text and verified back against it — all eighteen hold, checked twice by different readers. Every row's surrounding prose was corrected at least once; none passed clean, and the document says so. The one refusal type that could not be evidenced at all — the event that simply does not happen — is published as an empty row rather than removed from the chart, because an absence cannot be quoted and this dimension admits nothing it cannot quote.

Confidence

Eighteen of eighteen rows were CORRECTED; none passed clean and none failed. The pattern was uniform and is published in the document's own words: the quotation held in every case, the prose around it did not. False adjacency was the commonest defect — seven rows claimed two things happen close together when they do not, one of them across 2,380 words, and one 'twenty-eight lines later' that turned out to be an artifact of where the Gutenberg plaintext wraps rather than a fact about Joyce. **The most important limitation is structural.** One of the five refusal types — the event that simply does not happen — has zero rows, because an absence cannot be grepped and this dimension admits nothing it cannot quote. That empty category is a finding about the method before it is a finding about the book, it is drawn on the chart rather than removed from it, and any reader should discount the distribution accordingly. Two further negatives, stated because each would have been a strong result had it gone the other way: no episode declines to refuse, so the dimension cannot claim there is one place where Joyce keeps faith with Homer; and the Homeric column is a summary, not scholarship — this was built against the 1922 text, which the project holds, and not against a Greek text or any particular translation. Where a row depends on a fine point of the Odyssey rather than a broad one it is marked thin. A3 states are carried per row, which matters here because several Homeric-looking events in episode 15 occur only inside a hallucination.
