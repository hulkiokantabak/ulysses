The Governed City

DIMENSION 3 · TYPE I, INVENTORY · DIAGRAM REGISTER · THE UNIT: THE INSTRUMENT OF RULE

Thirteen instruments of rule — Castle, police, garrison, informers, courts, the language question and the rest — as they surface in the 1922 text. The reference maps held no tenement because the protagonists never enter one (catch C-03): rule here is ambient, not a place the plot visits. The device below shows it — the instruments spread thin across nearly every hour of the day, thickening only where the book itself turns on power. Quotes are verbatim from the public-domain text; states follow anchor A3.

The city is governed everywhere and administered nowhere the reader is taken: the marks fall in almost every episode, but the Castle, the courts and the barracks appear as reputation, salute, and memory far more often than as an act. Where rule finally becomes an event — the Citizen's nationalism in Cyclops, the hallucinated trial in Circe — the book has left realism behind.

The Dublin Metropolitan Police — the constable on his beat

the instruments

POLICE

The uniformed force is ambient regulation rather than event: it feeds and marches, salutes priest and viceroy, swaps gossip with undertakers, and is known by number like street furniture. Its one arrest of the day happens only in hallucination; at the real street violence it arrives late and is talked around by Corny Kelleher, while Bloom indicts it as perjured and class-serving, Gerty fears its station, and Molly imagines it being told on her.

The garrison — barracks, regiments, navy and the army as career

the instruments

BARRACKS-GARRISON

Present in every episode, the garrison is empire at its most ordinary: landlord (the Martello tower), employer and career (Tweedy, Seymour, Gardner, Bannon's cornetcy), spectacle (recruiting posters, Highland bands, sailor suits on four-year-olds), and sexual economy (the soldiers' walk, Monto beside the barracks). It frames the whole book — a War Office gun-tower at eight in the morning, Molly's Gibraltar of bugles and sentries at the end — and it delivers the only violence that actually lands all day: Private Carr's fist.

Dublin Castle and the offices of imperial administration

the instruments

CASTLE

The Castle almost never appears as a building and constantly as a network: landlord of the ground the book opens on, employer of cousins, paymaster of informers, source of authoritative knowledge ('We know that in the castle') and of quiet fixes (Mallon's hint). It is reached not through doors but through patronage — rent, departments lobbied, babies named for office-holding relatives, scandals hushed — rule as an office, mostly invisible.

The viceregal cavalcade

the instruments

CASTLE

The one daylight transit of the governing power through the governed city: announced by street placard at one, driven from Phoenix Park through the centre toward Ballsbridge at four while every citizen is catalogued by salute, snub or blindness, heard as ringing steel and a newspaper query at the Ormond, and burned off over Sandymount at nine as charity fireworks — then returning at midnight only as hallucination.

English law as the city's working machinery and favourite theatre, running the whole day: coroners and famous trials furnish the morning's talk, writs and the subsheriff actually seize and menace at three, the bench is mimicked in the pub at five, retold at the hospital at ten, hallucinated at midnight — and by night the apparatus is fully internalized, as Bloom's magistrate fantasy and penal taxonomy and Molly's contempt for the legal class.

What was left out

Deduplication folded recurring sightings into single instruments (Corny Kelleher's five appearances into the informer; Tweedy/Gibraltar into the garrison; Deasy's three surfacings split by function between schoolroom, Castle-administration and language). Deliberately excluded as instruments: the counter-city's own machinery — Fenian memory, Sinn Fein, rebel ballads, the nationalist statue gauntlet, the citizen — which the census recorded richly but which are instruments of resistance, not rule; they enter the selection only where rule touches them (the informer, the gallows, the anthem-language contest). Also excluded: foreign empires glimpsed in passing (the Turkish government of Agendath Netaim, the Austro-Hungarian viceconsulate, Bobrikoff's Russia, the French police of Stephen's Paris) as not governing Dublin; and singleton curios flagged in the census itself (the key to the tower, the lions couchant, the lost Armada) as emblems rather than instruments. Haines, the census's 'informal instrument,' is carried inside the language-of-rule entry (the collector of sayings) rather than given a slot. A3 registers (present / recalled / hallucinated) and A5 markings are preserved at census level and cited in the whys where load-bearing (Circe's Watch, Recorder, Rumbold and Edward VII are hallucinated; no execution or eviction is present-tense on 16 June 1904).
