Base document 02–03 · Place and hour

Two men crossing
one small city, once

Dublin, 8 a.m. Thursday 16 June to 2 a.m. Friday 17 June 1904. The gold thread is Bloom, the pale thread is Stephen. They come within a few metres of each other three times before noon and do not meet until ten at night.

Positions are plotted from real coordinates, so the distances are true — but the coastline, the river and the canals are drawn as a schematic, not a survey. The hours are Joyce's own, from the schemas he circulated privately; the minutes are reconstructions. Penelope has no hour at all, which is why Molly's band runs off the end of the clock and never closes.
