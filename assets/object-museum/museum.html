The Object Museum

DIMENSION 1 · TYPE I, INVENTORY · THE UNIT: THE OBJECT — POSITION HOUR BY HOUR, HAND TO HAND

Thirty-two objects, 301 stations, every station quoted from the 1922 text, which is in the public domain and quoted freely. States follow anchor A3 and are kept strictly apart: present — the object is in the scene; recalled — it exists here only in memory or thought; hallucinated — Circe's visions and dreams. Hours are approximate, taken from the itinerary's clock (base document 03), and say so. The census was of the whole text; the selection is thirty-two, not everything — the exclusions are stated at the end.

The test for a case in this museum is Braudel's: does the object carry a structure of the day — money, post, empire, memory, desire, the body — and does it move? Followed materially, the day is also a logistics: letters through grills, coins across counters, a cake of soap riding thirteen hours in a series of pockets, a house key absent from eight in the morning to two the next morning and governing everything in between.

What was left out

Left out, honestly: (1) single-scene food and tableware however vivid — the fry, the pork kidney, the gorgonzola sandwich, the crubeen and trotter, the Epps's cocoa — they feed one hour and carry structure already covered by the soap/potted-meat/basket cases; the kidney was the hardest cut (it opens Bloom's day and his carnivore-householder identity, but never leaves the kitchen). (2) Recalled-only keepsakes with no 16-June itinerary — the Claddagh ring (a superb hand-to-hand-to-grave chain, but entirely inside Molly's memory), Mulvey's handkerchief, Rudy's woolly jacket, the suicide note 'For my son Leopold' — the museum's present/recalled discipline (A3) argues they belong to a memory-wing, not object cases. (3) Hallucination-only props (Rudy's ivory cane, Bella's speaking Fan, the pandybat) — not in any room. (4) Episode-bound emblems that don't travel: the cracked lookingglass and shaving bowl (famous but confined to ep 1), the milkwoman's can, the tuningfork (lovely, but its structure — the forgotten object awaited by its owner — is carried by the stripling's cane), the Hely's sandwichboards, Molly's photo shown to Stephen, the coin flung from the Eccles street window to the onelegged sailor (a genuine 10→18 loop, cut at 32 with regret). (5) Stationary furniture-as-evidence and the ep-17 drawer archaeology (brass bed, rearranged sofa, daguerreotype, endowment policy) — profound strata, but they are the site the museum sits in rather than movers through it; the biscuit tin was retained as the sole single-episode exception because it is the day's one object turned weapon.

Caveats

  • An inventory is a reference, not an argument (the working document's own definition). The thirty-two are a selection under the museum test above; the text holds many more objects than any museum should.
  • Hours are approximate throughout — the itinerary's rule is that hours are firm and minutes are not; a station's hour places it in the day, no more.
  • Episode titles are schema scaffolding, not Joyce's (anchor A2).
  • The traces were extracted and then adversarially verified against the 1922 text; seventeen stations failed first verification and were corrected or amended before publication, and the corrections were verified again. Notes from that pass:
  • At quote boundaries, whole stage directions keep the text’s italic markers (_…_) under a stated, consistent policy.
  • Apostrophes inside quotes are set in the text’s curly form; quotes are otherwise verbatim.
  • Cyclops's gigantist interpolations and Molly's imagined futures strain the three A3 labels; where a station's reality is narratorial parody or projection rather than scene, memory, or vision, its what-column says so in words.
